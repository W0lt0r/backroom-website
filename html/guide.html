GUIDE





Intro

If you've found yourself in the Backrooms, don't panic. The environment may be unsettling, with its endless yellow walls, disorienting layout, and unnerving silence, but this guide is designed to help you navigate and, hopefully, escape.

Basic Survival

Water: Keep yourself hydrated. Occasionally, you may find water coolers. Make sure the water is clear before drinking. Food: Some rooms contain vending machines with non-perishable foods. Keep your energy up by eating sparingly.

Time Perception

Time may not function as you're used to in the Backrooms. Your phone or watch may malfunction. Try to keep a mental note of time, but be prepared for distortions.

Navigation

Markings: Always mark the rooms and hallways you've been in. Chalk or tape could be helpful for this. Maps: If possible, draw a quick map as you progress.

Types of Rooms

Safe Rooms: Usually empty and safe to rest in. Danger Zones: Rooms with mold, water leaks, or unsettling noises. Avoid these.

Levels

The Backrooms are often described as having different 'Levels,' each with its unique set of rules and landscapes.

Encountering Entities

You may not be alone. If you encounter any entities: +Do Not Make Eye Contact: Some entities react to sight. +Move Slowly: Sudden moves may provoke them.

Items to Carry

Flashlight: A must for darker rooms. First Aid Kit: For minor injuries. Portable Charger: Keep your devices charged.

Signs of an Exit

Some say exits do exist. Be on the lookout for: +Rooms with windows: Although rare, windows may mean you're close to an exit. +Strange frequencies on your phone: This could mean you're close to re-entering our reality.

Conclusion

Remember, the Backrooms are an ever-changing maze. There are no guaranteed methods of escape, but staying calm, being prepared, and following this guide may increase your chances of getting out.
